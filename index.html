Our Story

History Made Here.

Since
1969
◆ AVL 2026 ◆

It started with a fight. On June 28, 1969, trans women of color and drag performers refused to be pushed out of the Stonewall Inn in New York City. Their resistance ignited the modern LGBTQIA2+ rights movement — and the world has honored that courage every June since.

Asheville is one of the South's most forward-thinking cities. But Asheville has never had a June Pride festival to match that history. That changes June 26–28, 2026.

Grounded in the courage of those who came before — loud, proud, and not going anywhere.

AVL Stonewall Fest is a three-day outdoor celebration bringing together performers, artists, vendors, food, and the full fabric of this community. No gatekeeping. No watered-down vibes. Just real, raw, joyful pride in the mountains.
